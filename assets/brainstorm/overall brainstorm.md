## Basic info
Name: Anatolia
Setting: Set in Early Iron Age near east. There's a big, ancient dungeon within Gobekli Tepe that we find ourselves sucked into, forever (basically isekai'd)



## Character backstory
- archeologist?

## Overall design themes
- juxtaposition between:
	- high level of "real-worldness" and "historic-sounding" flavor x nonsensical elements
	- ample exposition and description x convoluted systems
- constantly leaves you wondering, damn why tf do they know that or damn why tf did they bother to include that
- urgency in gameplay
- repeatability / extensibility of items, enemies, skills, etc. within a fairly low-level (close to the physics as possible)
	- everything still feels handmade
- focus on backend engine before gameplay loop

## Starter gameplay
- text based MVP
- autobattle style combat
- gauntlet of enemies (randomized)
- heal after each 3 rounds (the Ancient Megalith shines brightly upon you, healing your wounds)
